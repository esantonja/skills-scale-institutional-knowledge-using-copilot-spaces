## Overview of OctoAcme Project Management Processes

OctoAcme employs a structured, cross-functional project management methodology grounded in core principles: prioritizing customer value, iterative delivery, and clear ownership. Projects begin with an initiation phase using a Project One-pager to define goals, business needs, stakeholders, and success metrics; this sets the foundation for planning, execution, and improvement.

Key roles drive the process: Product Managers set outcomes and backlog priorities; Project Managers coordinate delivery, risks, and communication; Developers implement features and maintain quality; QA/testers validate acceptance criteria and feedback. Each role’s responsibilities are clearly defined, facilitating seamless coordination and minimizing ambiguity across phases of initiation, planning, execution, release, and retrospectives.

Workflows leverage agile principles, with project boards tracking stages such as Backlog, In Progress, QA, and Done. PR conventions require small, reviewable changes linked to issues with defined acceptance criteria, supported by automated testing, linting, and security scans. Quality assurance is embedded, requiring unit, integration, and end-to-end tests, manual QA for acceptance, and risk management via a risk register with clear escalation paths. Continuous improvement is achieved through sprint and incident retrospectives, with learnings and action items tracked in the backlog.

Communication is frequent and structured: daily standups for progress and blockers, weekly delivery syncs for status and risks, stakeholder updates and demos for visibility, and retrospectives to capture learning. This approach ensures reliable, impactful products, fosters transparency, and enables consistent execution and adaptation.
